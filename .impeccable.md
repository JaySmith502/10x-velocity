## Design Context

### Users
Mid-market executives (VP/Director-level) at companies with 50-500 employees, evaluating AI and automation vendors. They arrive via search, referral, or physical marketing materials. They're decision-makers with budgets — not browsing, they're evaluating. Secondary audience includes nonprofit leaders seeking operational efficiency. These buyers need to feel confident this firm can deliver before they'll book a call.

### Brand Personality
**3 words:** Confident, energetic, approachable

A blend of warmth and momentum. 10x Velocity should feel like the smartest person in the room who's also genuinely easy to work with. Not cold or corporate. Not hype or startup-bro. Authoritative expertise delivered with human warmth and forward energy.

**Emotional goal:** Within 3 seconds, a visitor should think: "These people know what they're doing."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Tone:** Premium-approachable — Stripe's polish meets Apple's confidence, with warmth that Notion brings. Refined but not sterile. Energetic but not loud.

**Theme:** Light mode primary, dark mode supported via toggle. Light mode is the default — it stands out from the sea of dark AI sites and reads as more established/professional to executive buyers.

**References:**
- Stripe / Notion — refined, content-rich, professional but not cold
- Apple / Porsche — premium positioning, cinematic quality, confidence

**Anti-references:**
- The current site's Lovable scaffold aesthetic
- Cyan-on-dark AI template look
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, blur orbs, identical card grids
- Any pattern that reads as "AI generated this"

### Brand Colors (from physical marketing materials — must keep)
- **Cyan:** #33C3F0 — use as a sharp accent, not the dominant color
- **Purple/Magenta:** #D043E6 — secondary accent for energy and differentiation
- These should punctuate, not saturate. The palette needs warm, tinted neutrals as the foundation.

### Design Principles
1. **Authority through restraint** — Competence is shown through precision, not decoration. Every element earns its place. White space is confidence.
2. **Results over promises** — Lead with proof (case studies, numbers, outcomes), not generic value propositions. The design should make data and results feel prominent and trustworthy.
3. **Warmth at scale** — Premium doesn't mean cold. Photography, human language, and approachable typography keep the brand feeling like real people, not a faceless consultancy.
4. **Intentional energy** — The name is "10x Velocity." Motion, typography weight, and layout should convey forward momentum without resorting to gimmicks. Speed is felt through efficiency of design, not through animation quantity.
5. **Light by default** — A light, airy foundation signals establishment and professionalism to executive buyers. Dark mode is an option, not the identity.
