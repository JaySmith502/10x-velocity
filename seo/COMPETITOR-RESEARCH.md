## Part 1: Competitor Profiles

### NATIONAL / NICHE

#### 1. RTS Labs (rtslabs.com — homepage + /ai-costs-and-roi)
- **Positioning:** "You don't need another AI strategy. You need the thing *built*." Self-described "boutique applied AI consulting firm. Small by choice, senior by design." Targets high-growth/enterprise; verticals: logistics, insurance, real estate/construction, finance.
- **Region:** National (Richmond, VA; "100% US Based Employees").
- **Services:** AI consulting/agents, data engineering, software engineering, platforms (Azure, Salesforce, Snowflake, AWS).
- **Published pricing:** Yes — strongest cost content in the set. Cost table: assessment $10K–$15K, project development $50K–$140K, customization/integration $50K–$70K, support $10K–$50K; plus prose ranges ("$7,000 to $35,000" for assessment, "$100,000 to $500,000 or more" for custom models, "$21,000 to $70,000" annual maintenance). **The table and prose contradict each other** (assessment $10–15K vs $7–35K) — exploitable.
- **FAQ/question content:** Six PAA-style Q&A H4s, TL;DR block, ToC. FAQ answers are vague ("costs vary depending on several factors") — they don't restate the numbers.
- **Trust signals:** Heavy. Named clients (WEX, CarMax, Dominion Energy, Advance Auto Parts, Goodwill, Landstar), case-study numbers ("integration time dropped from six weeks to four days," "23% increase in net profit"), 10+ years, 200+ projects, named testimonials. Cost article dated June 2024, modified Jan 2025 — aging; blog fresh (June 2026).
- **AEO read:** Strong on numbers + formatting + proof. Weak: enterprise skew, internal cost contradictions, cost page aging, zero mid-market or nonprofit framing.

#### 2. Iternal Technologies (iternal.ai/ai-consulting)
- **Positioning:** "AI Consulting in 2026: What It Is, How It Works & What It Costs" — deliberately AEO-engineered pillar. Wedge: sovereign/private/air-gapped AI. Target: enterprise, gov/defense, regulated; "fractional CAIO" pitched at mid-market.
- **Region:** National/global.
- **Published pricing:** Most explicit in set: "$100–$1,200+ per hour, $10K–$5M+ per project, or $2K–$150K per month on retainer"; boutique-vs-Big-4 table; fixed tiers: Masterclass $2,497; AI Strategy Sprint $50,000; Transformation Program $150,000; Founder's Circle $750K–$1.5M.
- **FAQ/question content:** Best-in-class AEO formatting: answer-first bolded definitional sentences under every H2, a literal "semantic summary" blockquote, 7-question FAQ, TL;DR, cited stats (RAND 80% failure, MIT NANDA 95%). Authored, dated June 6, 2026, byline with credentials.
- **Trust signals:** Author E-E-A-T but **no named clients and no verifiable case studies** on the page; "Trusted by global leaders" shows only "GovernmentAcquisitions". Scarcity gimmicks ("Limited to 6 engagements per year").
- **AEO read:** The page to beat for "AI consulting cost." Weakness: useless-wide ranges for a mid-market buyer, enterprise/sovereign lens, thin proof, nothing local or SMB-practical.

## Part 3: High-Intent Questions Competitors Answer Poorly or Not at All

Ranked by gap size × intent × winnability for a lower-authority domain.

### A. Honest pricing for mid-market (biggest gap)
1. "How much does AI consulting cost for a mid-market company (50–500 employees)?" — Iternal's range is uselessly wide; RTS contradicts itself; locals publish nothing. Win: one declarative page, narrow honest table by engagement type, "what changes the price," worked example.
2. "What should an AI readiness assessment cost?" — only MLDeep names a number ($15K), data-eng scope only.
3. "Retainer vs project pricing for mid-market" / "hidden costs of AI implementation" — nobody quantifies for mid-market.
4. "How much does Copilot implementation cost for a 100-person company?" — all three local MSPs sell it; none publishes a price or licensing math. Zero competition.

### B. AI readiness for 50–500 employee companies
5. "AI readiness assessment without a data team" — MLDeep assumes dbt/IaC/evals; Xcelacore is unquantified and aimed at 12-employee shops. The QuickBooks + CRM + SharePoint org has no framework. Win: scored rubric calibrated to mid-market realities — LLMs cite rubric tables.
6. "Signs your company is NOT ready for AI" — only Xcelacore touches it, without thresholds.
7. "AI readiness for nonprofits" — completely unserved by all 10 competitors.

### C. Change management / AI adoption (systematically neglected)
8. "How do you get employees to actually use AI tools?" — Iternal name-drops the 10-20-70 rule and sells a book; RTS gives one FAQ line; locals offer "training" as a bullet. No substantive citable answer exists. Pairs with the "95% of pilots fail" stat everyone quotes and nobody operationalizes.
9. "Will AI replace my employees?" — only Z-JAK answers, in two sentences.
10. "Why do AI pilots fail at mid-sized companies?" — failure stats are enterprise-cited; nobody translates failure modes to the 50–500 segment.

### D. Louisville / Kentucky local
11. "AI consulting in Louisville — who does it and what does it cost?" — no Louisville page combines AI specifics + pricing + FAQ + named local proof. A complete guide, even one that fairly names competitors, would be the most complete answer in existence.
12. "AI consulting for mid-market companies in Kentucky" — Z-JAK caps at 150; Louisville Geek is MSP-generic; Centric is enterprise. Literally no targeted page exists.
13. Vertical × local ("AI automation for Kentucky logistics/healthcare/manufacturing") — nothing exists for Louisville metro verticals (UPS Worldport logistics, healthcare, manufacturing, bourbon/hospitality).
14. "Louisville AI consultant vs national firm" — UPTech makes the argument for Central KY; nobody makes it for Louisville, and nobody makes it honestly.

### E. How to choose / vet
15. "How to choose an AI consulting firm (not an enterprise)" — RTS: three generic sentences; Iternal: strong but self-serving and enterprise-criteria'd; Xcelacore's 15-question checklist is the closest competitor, agency-written, no numbers. Win: mid-market-specific criteria + red-flag pricing thresholds + scorecard.
16. "AI consultant vs MSP — what's the difference?" — both Louisville incumbents are MSPs bolting AI onto managed IT. The buyer's guide distinguishing MSP Copilot rollout from process-redesign AI consulting reframes the local set in 10x Velocity's favor.

### Format playbook (what's winning citations now)
Answer-first bolded definition sentences under question H2s (Iternal); a real pricing table (RTS, Iternal); a scoring rubric table (MLDeep); dated, bylined, credentialed authorship (Iternal, MLDeep); FAQ blocks with self-contained answers (Z-JAK, UPTech); named clients with one hard number each (RTS). The single highest-leverage move: every target question gets a page whose first 50 words fully answer it with a number in them — across all 13 pages scraped, only Iternal and MLDeep do that today, and neither serves mid-market Louisville.
