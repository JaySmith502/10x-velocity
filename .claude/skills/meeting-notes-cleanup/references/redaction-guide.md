# Redaction Guide — Internal vs. External Recaps

When a recap is going outside the note-taker's team, raw notes usually contain content that's fine for an internal Slack channel but damaging in a client's inbox. This guide lists what to cut, what to keep, and how to soften sensitive content without losing the underlying information.

## What to cut

**Emotional reads of attendees.** Anything about how someone seemed to feel.

- "Diane got tense when it came up" → cut entirely
- "Marcus was frustrated" → cut, or replace with factual equivalent if the frustration was the substance ("Marcus noted ongoing issues with…")
- "Facilities coordinator didn't say much but nodded a lot" → cut
- "Harder to read" / "seemed excited" / "seemed uncomfortable" → cut

**Internal strategy.** How the note-taker plans to handle the relationship.

- "Don't push on budget next call" → cut
- "Watch the dynamic with Diane" → cut
- "Don't bring up X unless they do" → cut
- "Marcus is a champion" → cut
- "Read the room" → cut

**Non-verbal / tonal observations.**

- "He lowered his voice when he said it" → cut
- "Kind of tense" / "awkward pause" → cut

**Unnamed or unflattering characterizations.**

- "The guy whose name I never got" → either confirm name and use it, or drop to "A facilities coordinator from Acme"
- "The one who just nodded" → cut or neutralize

**Hedged guesses without context.** When the raw note is a guess the note-taker isn't confident in.

- "~30 techs but probably more like 22-25" → move to Items to Confirm as "Total technician headcount (initial estimate: 22–30, including part-time)"
- "400-600 work orders per month, rough guess" → same treatment

## What to keep

- All factual content about current state, systems, volumes, process
- All decisions and action items
- Attendee names and titles (with spelling flagged in Items to Confirm if uncertain)
- Dates and timelines (with flags if uncertain)
- Scope and budget status in neutral terms ("Budget has been approved for this quarter" is fine to include if the other party said it — just don't include the note-taker's read on it)
- Compliance / regulatory topics mentioned by the other party (softened — see below)

## Softening patterns

When the raw note is honest but sharp, neutralize without erasing.

| Raw note | External-safe |
|---|---|
| "Documentation gaps ahead of Q3 inspection" | "Q3 compliance timeline was discussed; to be revisited in follow-up" |
| "They're behind on X" | "X was noted as an open workstream" |
| "Process is a mess" | "Current process has known inefficiencies that motivated the engagement" |
| "No one updates the sheet" | "The shared tracker has not been consistently maintained" |
| "They don't know what CMMS they have" | "Name of current CMMS to be confirmed" |
| "Accounting seemed nervous about it" | Cut entirely, or "Topic flagged for follow-up with appropriate stakeholders" |

The pattern: describe the situation, not the person's reaction to it.

## Format-for-internal vs. format-for-external

Ask the user (or infer) which audience. When in doubt, produce external-safe and tell the user what you cut so they can add it back for an internal version.

If the user wants both versions, produce two files:

- `meeting-recap-internal.docx` — includes the note-taker's read-the-room observations and strategy notes
- `meeting-recap-external.docx` — redacted per this guide

## Judgment calls to flag

Always mention these to the user in the delivery message, not buried in the doc:

- Content you cut for audience reasons ("Removed internal commentary about…")
- Topics you softened ("Softened the Q3 inspection language to neutral framing")
- Uncertainties moved to Items to Confirm ("Moved date, headcount, and CMMS name to Items to Confirm")

This gives the user a chance to override before the doc goes out.
